Form I-485
Application to Register Permanent Residence or Adjust Status
✓ Analyzed

📖 Simple English Summary

Key points explained in plain language — perfect for beginner English speakers.

📚 Reading Level: Grade 5  ·  Beginner Friendly

📝 What This Document Says

  • This is a form to apply for a Green Card (permanent residence) in the United States.
  • You must already be inside the United States to use this form.
  • You need to have a visa number available for your category.
  • You must read all instructions carefully before filling out the form.
  • You must pay a filing fee — this fee cannot be returned to you.
  • Keep copies of everything you submit for your own records.
  • USCIS is the government agency that reviews your application.

📚 Words to Know

Lawful Permanent Resident
A person allowed to live and work in the US permanently. Also called a "Green Card holder."
Adjust Status
Changing your immigration status while you remain inside the United States.
Immigrant Visa Number
A number showing there is a Green Card available for you right now. The "Visa Bulletin" shows if your number is ready.
Priority Date
The date your visa petition was filed. It determines your place in line for a Green Card.
USCIS
U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services — the government office that processes immigration applications.
Filing Fee
The money you pay when submitting your form. It is not returned even if the application is denied.

💡 What This Means For You

This form — Form I-485 — is how you apply for your Green Card without leaving the United States. A Green Card means you can live and work in the US permanently. You fill out this form, pay the fee, and USCIS will review your application. They may schedule an interview with you. This process takes time, so save copies of everything.
